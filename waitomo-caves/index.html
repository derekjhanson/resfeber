Waitomo Caves

The stars glisten a calming shade of light blue, sitting at the expanse of your vision. As your guide slowly paddles the boat through the gentle waters, a cool breeze gently sweeps across the bow as everyone silently stares at the expanse.

The tiny lights in the distance appear similar to our celestial night sky to the naked eye at water level, but closer inspection brings a host of surprises. Far from stars, those calm lights are instead part of the ruse of a fisherman on the ceiling, looking for dinner.

It truly is a sight to see, thousands of glow worms attached to the ceiling, each with their own plot to fish from. Tourists come from the world over to take a guided tour in one of several locations throughout New Zealand, though the Waitomo caves are the most well-known. 

The bioluminescent glow may be the highlight of most cave tours, but a guided walk through a separate cave offers an up-close look at the lines these creatures lay to catch their prey.

To learn more about these magnificent creatures, and see them in action, check out the video: https://vimeo.com/356649644.

Ruakuri Cave

After the classic boat tour through the caverns filled with glow worms we had the chance to explore a separate cave on foot. Filled with incredible formations, from drapery folds, to popcorn, to entire ceilings of stalactites, this second cave provided some of the most beautiful natural cave art we've seen.
