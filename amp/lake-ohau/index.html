Lake Ohau

By DJH July 1, 2019

After driving for hours it looks like Oahu. There isn't a single palm tree in sight, but the sign for Lake Ohau sure had us thinking about Hawaii. The famous lakes of Aoraki / Mt. Cook needed our attention first, but something about a mysterious lake invisible from the highway lingered in our dreams that night in Twizel.

Maybe it was the frigid temperature in our box without heat. Maybe it was the shivering and rustling in our blankets, trying to ward off frozen toes and icy noses. Whatever the cause, the association of a mysterious lake with the warm tropical weather of Hawaii stirred our desires the next morning.

To say Lake Ohau is off the beaten path is an understatement. The fact that a sign exists, with several options for your traveling delights, seems to imply desperation for visitors. But with services beyond sight (17 km to the start of the lake, more if the ski resort is your end destination), few appear to ever set eyes on the lake.

The drive rolls gently over hills of golden grasslands, pulling the distant mountains closer to view with each passing dash of the roadway. After a few kilometers, we were satisfied with the mountain views, evoking the splendor of California's coastal mountains or the golden, dusty hue of the drive towards Death Valley.

Suddenly, the road both bends and drops, giving you the first glimpse of the lake and leaving you begging for more.

Framed by mountains on three sides and the valley you arrive in on the fourth, the desolate lake is also windswept. Wave after wave whipsaws the surface of Lake Ohau, pelting onlookers with a barrage of fresh air. Or, in winter, a breeze making you wonder whether it is possible to wear three gloves at once and appreciative of that hot cup of coffee you brought with you.

The rocks here lining the shore are ----,

You can imagine them tumbling down with the melting glaciers and tumbling against one another in the endless waves, coming to their rounded state quicker than many of the calmer glacial lakes of the world, or so it seems.

The road continues along the western, then northern side of the lake offering a few camping pullouts for presumably warmer days before morphing into a steep climb zig-zagging up the mountain range towards the ski resort at the top.

Looking back towards the valley, the aquamarine color of the lake is even more evident. The suns rays glistening off the 'rock flour' present in the water give a tint or hue spectacular even in comparison to the many beautiful lakes of the region.

As if the surprise needed more enjoyment, the drive back to the highway gave constant reminders of the valley's beauty in the mirrors.
